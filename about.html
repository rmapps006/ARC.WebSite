The Founder

It Began with One Hammer

In 1966, Abdul Rashid set up a small workshop in the Kasera Bazar of Lahore’s Walled City — the copper quarter, where the sound of hammer on metal has echoed for centuries. With little more than a set of stakes and an unshakeable eye for form, he began shaping copper the way his own teachers had.

What started as a single stall became a name that families across Punjab came to trust for cookware that would outlive them.

“

A piece of copper remembers every hand that shaped it. We simply make sure those hands belong to a master.

— The Abdul Rashid Family
Our Heritage

Rooted in Lahore

The Walled City

Our workshop still stands where it always has — in the historic copper bazaar, surrounded by the craftsmen and traditions that shaped it.

Skill, Handed Down

Every technique we use was taught by the generation before — son learning from father, at the same anvil, for sixty years and counting.

Our Mission

To keep the art of handmade copper alive — and to place a piece of Lahore’s heritage in homes around the world.
